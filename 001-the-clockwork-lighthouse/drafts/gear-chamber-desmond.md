The chamber opens like the inside of a skull.

You stand at the threshold of something vast and wrong. Gears rise around you — not arranged but *assembled*, a vertical cathedral of iron and brass that stretches upward into shadow. Cogs the size of millwheels interlock with smaller mechanisms, chains droop between them like the ligaments of some enormous skeleton. The air smells of cold metal and ancient grease.

Everything is still.

That is the wrongness. This was meant to *move*. You can feel it in the teeth of every gear, in the taut slack of every chain — a machine designed for perpetual motion, frozen mid-breath. Dust has settled into the grooves of cogs like sediment in dead riverbeds.

One gear, larger than the rest, dominates the chamber's heart. It is seized — locked at some impossible angle, weeping dark grease from its hub in slow, black tears. Around its circumference, numbers have been etched with surgical precision. They count down. You watch them: 00:03:42. Then 00:03:41.

A brass plate is bolted to the wall, its engraving sharp despite the years:

**AT ZERO — THE LIGHT DIES.**

You look up. Above the mechanism, a shaft rises through the ceiling toward the lantern room far above. Whatever light this lighthouse once cast, this machine was its heartbeat. And now the heart is stopping.

In the center of the seized gear's axle, a socket gapes — hexagonal, deep, sized for something specific. A crank-handle, perhaps. The kind you'd use to wind a mechanism back from the dead.

The grease on the seized gear is thick and black, but not impenetrable. Old. Dried. Oil could loosen what time has welded shut — free the gear, expose the socket's full depth, accept a crank. And if the crank turned — you can almost hear it: a groan of iron waking, chains snapping taut, the great gears grinding into motion like the bones of a titan remembering how to walk. The numbers would stop their terrible descent. The light would hold.

00:03:36.

But that future is not yet written. The numbers keep falling. Whatever happens at zero, the brass plate promises nothing good.

This is a machine that does not forgive stillness.
