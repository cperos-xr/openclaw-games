You haul yourself through the hatch and the sky hits you — vast, black, salt-stinging, open on three sides like a jaw unhinged. Wind screams through the iron railings. The fourth wall is glass, thick and ancient, spiderwebbed with fractures that catch the light wrong.

The light. God, the light.

It burns above the brass pedestal in a great faceted lens, but there is no warmth in it. No fire. It is a mechanical thing — blue-white, buzzing, insectile — a cold star caged in glass. The beam rotates with a grinding pulse, sweeping the churning black sea where no ship will ever sail again. You can hear the mechanism below, through the glass floor panels: a cathedral of gears turning, turning, turning the island into the ocean.

The countdown on the wall reads 00:00:12.

Then the air goes thin. A shape condenses between you and the lamp — not blocking the light but *bending* it, edges fraying like wet paper. Silas. What remains of Silas. His face is gaunt, translucent, eyes hollowed by a century of watching this machine eat his world. He reaches out and his fingers trail blue phosphor.

He is not angry. He is *terrified.*

"Turn the key." His voice scrapes like rust on iron. "Reset the light. Before the sea takes it all."

You feel the clockwork-key heavy in your hand — heavy with purpose, heavy with every room you've bled through to reach this moment. The pedestal waits: brass, ornate, its keyhole shaped like the hands of a clock frozen at midnight.

You drive the key in. Turn.

The mechanism below *shrieks.* Gears reverse. The whole lighthouse shudders like a living thing drawing breath after drowning. The blue-white light flickers, gutters — and then *blooms.* Golden. Warm. The color of sunrise over safe harbor. The beam steadies, sweeps gentle and amber across water that has stopped clawing at the walls.

Through the glass floor, the gears slow. The island holds.

Silas watches the warm light wash across his spectral hands. Something in his face unknots — a century of grief dissolving. He looks at you, and for the first time, he smiles.

"Thank you."

He fades. Not into darkness. Into the light.

The lighthouse breathes. The sea is calm. You are alive.
