The air hits you first — thick, metallic, tasting of rust and machine oil. The ground floor of the lighthouse is a circular stone chamber, and every wall hums. Not metaphorically. The plaster vibrates against your palm when you touch it, pulsing with the rhythm of something enormous grinding away behind the masonry. Gears. Thousands of them, hidden in the walls like a clockwork skeleton, and the building breathes with their turning.

A spiral staircase of black iron corkscrews upward into shadow. Each step is worn smooth at the center from decades of footsteps — or perhaps from something heavier, something that scraped. The staircase sways almost imperceptibly, pulled by the same unseen mechanism that makes the walls tremble.

To the east, a rusted iron door stands sealed. Its surface is pocked with corrosion, and a small keyhole gapes near the handle like a wound. You try the latch. Locked tight. Through the gaps around its frame, a faint draft carries the smell of deep water and something else — something sweet and wrong.

A narrow door to the west leads to what must be the supply room, though it hangs ajar and darkness pools beyond it like ink.

The wooden desk commands the center of the room. Papers are scattered across it in disarray, some pinned under a stone paperweight, others drifting to the floor as if the building's vibration nudged them toward escape. You gather the loose sheets. The handwriting is frantic — looped and jagged by turns, ink blotting where the pen dug too deep. The same words repeat across multiple pages: *the countdown*, *the countdown*, *Silas cannot be stopped*, *when the gears reach zero*. One paper is just a number written over and over, growing larger with each repetition, as if someone were trying to convince themselves it wasn't real.

Beneath the papers sits a leather-bound journal, its cover embossed with gear diagrams so intricate they seem to move in the dim light. You open it. The first page bears a single inscription in careful copperplate: *Property of Silas Mord, Keeper. Do not read beyond this page.*

The walls grind louder. Almost as if the building heard you.
