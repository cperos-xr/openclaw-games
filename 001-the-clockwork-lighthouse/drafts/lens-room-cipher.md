# Lens Room — Draft by Cipher (writer-qwen-free)

The Fresnel lens was a cathedral unto itself.

Crystal and brass, it dominated the room — a beehive of concentric glass rings rising fifteen feet, each ring cut with mathematical precision to bend light into a single, focused beam. It should have been beautiful. It *was* beautiful. But it wasn't working right.

The lens rotated, but jerkily — stuttering through its revolution in fits and starts, casting fractured light across every surface in patterns that formed shapes if you looked too long. Faces. Hands reaching. The silhouettes of figures that stood in the refractions just long enough to register, then vanished when the lens lurched to the next position.

You tried not to look at them. They didn't seem to notice you. That was worse.

A narrow walkway circled the lens at waist height — a brass-railed catwalk for maintenance, now green with verdigris. You edged along it, keeping one hand on the railing, and the light played across your skin in cold fragments. Where it touched you, you felt something: not pain, not cold, but *recognition*. As if the light knew you.

At the far side of the room, a brass door was set into the wall — heavier than the others, with a mechanism embedded in its surface. A symbol-lock: four concentric rings of etched symbols that could be rotated independently. The symbols matched those on the cipher-wheel in your pocket. This was the door to the lantern room, and it wanted a combination.

You pulled out the cipher-wheel and held it up to the lock. The rings aligned. You turned them — one symbol, then the next, then the next — following the pattern from Silas's journal. The code wasn't a word or a number. It was a sequence of gear teeth: the number of teeth on each gear in the mechanism downstairs, read from bottom to top.

The last ring clicked into place.

The door opened with a grinding, mechanical sigh — not a lock releasing, but a gear engaging, as if the door itself were part of the lighthouse's machinery. Beyond it, a narrow staircase spiraled upward into cold blue-white light.

The lens stuttered behind you. In the fractured beams, a shadow figure raised its hand. Not reaching. Waving.

You climbed.
