# Dock — Draft by Desmond Vex (writer-deepseek)

The motorboat bumped against the posts with a hollow, waterlogged sound. You stepped onto the dock and felt the timbers shift beneath your weight — not a welcome, but a warning.

The island rose above you like a broken tooth. Fifty feet of sheer black rock, slick with spray, crowned by the lighthouse. Its beam swept out across the churning waves in long, erratic arcs — one slow pass, then a stutter, then a frantic spin before settling into rhythm again. At the top, something moved against the light. A shape. A figure, maybe. Then the beam swept on and it was gone.

You told yourself it was a bird.

The dock groaned again, lower this time, and you looked down. The waterline had crept past the high-tide mark. Past the barnacle line. Past the rust stain on the pilings where the ocean hadn't reached in years. The tide wasn't just high — it was *wrong*. The island was sinking, or the sea was rising, and the difference didn't matter much from where you stood.

Behind you, the waves chewed at the rocks with a sound like grinding teeth. Ahead, a stone staircase was carved into the cliff face — old, uneven, slick with spray. It switchbacked up toward the lighthouse in tight hairpins, vanishing into shadow where the cliff overhung.

The sky above was the color of a bruise. Purple-grey clouds scudded low, torn by a wind that carried salt and something else — oil, maybe. Old machinery. The smell of metal left to rust.

The lighthouse beam swept across you. For one freezing instant, the light held steady on the dock, and you saw your shadow stretch long and thin across the rotting planks. Then it stuttered and moved on, searching the sea for something it never found.

Something at the top of the light flickered. A shape in the window. Watching.

You started up the stairs.
