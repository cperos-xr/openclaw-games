The chamber breathes.

Not with air — with *steel*. You step through the hatch and the sheer *scale* of it hits you like a fist to the sternum. Gears the size of houses, cogs interlocking in chains that climb toward darkness, a mechanical cathedral built by something that understood rotation the way priests understand prayer. The walls are round. Everything is round. The lighthouse *is* this room, and this room is a clock, and the clock is dying.

Most of the mechanism has stopped. Rust fuses teeth to teeth. Chains hang slack like severed tendons. But the main gear — the enormous central wheel — still *moves*. Barely. A slow, agonized crawl, each tooth catching and slipping, shedding black grease that pools on the stone below like old blood.

You read the numbers etched around its rim as it turns. They're counting down.

**00:03:42.**

A brass plate, bolted to the axle housing, spells it out in no uncertain terms: **AT ZERO — THE LIGHT DIES.**

Your gaze follows the axle upward and finds it: a socket, hexagonal, sized for a crank-handle. The kind you turn to keep a machine honest.

But one massive cog in the lower train has *seized*. Teeth locked against its neighbor, warped from heat or age. Old grease weeps from the join in long, slow ribbons. Until that gear is freed, the whole mechanism chokes on itself.

You can feel the countdown in your teeth. The lighthouse is a wound that hasn't finished bleeding.

**00:03:31.**

The oil finds the seized gear like blood finding a wound. It seeps into the warped teeth, black and thick, and for a moment nothing happens — just the countdown grinding forward, **00:01:18**, **00:01:17** —

Then a *crack*. Metal screaming against metal as the frozen cog lurches, breaks free, and begins to turn. You jam the crank-handle into the socket and *lean into it*. The whole chamber shudders. Chains snap taut. Gears that have been dead for decades groan awake, teeth meshing with a sound like a cathedral organ built from iron. The vibration climbs through the floor, up your legs, into your chest where it settles as a second heartbeat.

The numbers around the main gear slow. **00:00:44... 00:00:44... 00:00:44.**

Stabilized. The clockwork breathes again — not with relief, but with *hunger*. Something ancient and patient has been wound.

The light above flickers, steadies, *burns*.

You have bought time. Nothing more.
