The room breathes.

Not metaphorically — the circular stone walls expand and contract by fractions of millimeters, pushed from within by something vast and toothed. The plaster has cracked in places, revealing not brick but *teeth*: interlocking brass cogs the size of dinner plates, spinning in a rhythm too slow for machines, too steady for anything living. A hum saturates the air. Not heard so much as *inherited*, passed through your sternum like a second heartbeat the building insists you adopt.

You stand in the ground floor of the lighthouse, though "floor" suggests something passive. This is a throat. The walls curve inward, slick with condensation that smells of copper and old oil, and above you a spiral staircase coils upward like the interior of a nautilus shell — if nautilus shells were made of cast iron and someone had left them out in a century of rain. Each step is bowed slightly, as though the staircase is slowly digesting something heavy that climbed it long ago.

To the east, an iron door sits in the stone like an abscess. Rust has fused its hinges into a single red mass. It will not open. It will not open *yet*. There is a difference, and the machinery seems to know it.

A narrow door west leads somewhere dark and close — a supply room, by the shape of it, though supplies for what?

Your attention falls, inevitably, on the desk. Wooden, scarred, shoved against the curve of the wall as if trying to escape. Papers litter its surface like shed skin — diagrams of gears within gears, spirals annotated in a hand that starts precise and ends *scrawled*. One phrase repeats across three separate sheets, each iteration larger than the last, each more frantic:

*THE COUNTDOWN. THE COUNTDOWN. THE COUNTDOWN.*

Beneath the papers, a leather-bound journal. Its cover is embossed with a gear-and-star sigil, and the spine crackles when you touch it — not from age, but from tension, as though the book has been holding its breath.

The name *Silas* appears on the first visible page, circled seven times in red ink, the final circle drawn so hard it tore through.

The walls pulse. The gears grind. Somewhere above you, in the dark throat of the staircase, something resets with a sound like a jawbone snapping shut.

The countdown is already underway.
