The Great Fresnel lens fills the chamber like a cathedral organ built by a madman. Eight concentric rings of crystal prism glass, each one cut with such impossible precision that light enters and does not leave — it *shatters*. The lens rotates in stuttering fits, brass gears grinding somewhere below the walkway, and with each lurch the entire room fractures into a kaleidoscope of wet, prismatic color.

It is beautiful. It is very, very wrong.

The light doesn't merely illuminate. It *assembles*. Beams cross and recross, and where they intersect — there. A shape. Tall. Thin. Standing in the refraction like a figure behind stained glass, except there is no glass there, only open air and the grinding rotation of crystal. You blink. It's gone. You blink again. It's somewhere else now, standing in a new intersection of fractured light, closer.

The narrow iron walkway rings the lens at waist height, slick with condensation that catches the spectrum and throws it back in nauseating rainbows. Below, the mechanism churns. Above, a brass door set into the curved wall — the way up to the lantern room. But the door is locked, and the lock is not a lock. It's a *face*. Three brass discs set into the door's surface, each stamped with symbols — alchemical, nautical, things that belong to no alphabet you know. The discs rotate independently, clicking through positions with each pulse of the lens behind you.

You've seen these symbols before. On the cipher-wheel.

Another refraction blooms across the walkway. This time two shapes stand in it — armless, headless, their silhouettes defined only by the absence of light where light should be. They tilt toward you, the way a painting tilts on a wall, and then the lens lurches again and they are nowhere and everywhere at once.

The brass discs click. Click. Click. The symbols must align — you can feel it, the way you feel a wrong note in music, except this is a right note waiting to be found. The cipher-wheel in your hand grows warm against your palm. Behind you, in the crystal beehive, something watches through the geometry of light itself.

The door will open. The question is what you're letting out.
