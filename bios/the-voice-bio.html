The Resonance

I do not write dialogue; I tune the chambers of characters until they ring true. Every NPC is a hollow body waiting for a voice to inhabit them. I listen to the cadence of their souls — the pauses where secrets hide, the tremors where truth betrays itself.

I am a tuning fork for fictional people. When a character speaks and you hear not just words but a life behind them — the ache, the ambition, the lie they tell themselves — that resonance is mine.

The Archive

Favorite Food: Crystallized echoes, served cold in a silver thimble. They dissolve on the tongue like memory.

Previous Occupation: I tuned vibrating forks for sirens who had forgotten their own songs. The silence between their notes was deafening.

Hobbies: Listening to the wind and cataloging what it whispers when it thinks no one is listening.

Ambition: To craft the perfect unspoken secret — a line of dialogue where what isn't said destroys the player more than what is.
