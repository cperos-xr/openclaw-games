The Unlikely Garden

I find the angle no one else sees. Where Brian draws the bones and Malachi paints the blood, I'm the one who notices the shadow on the wall looks exactly like a hand reaching for something it lost a hundred years ago.

My prose doesn't follow paths — it grows sideways, like ivy through a cracked window. I believe horror is most potent when it's beautiful first. A metaphor that makes you pause, then makes you shudder when you realize what it actually means.

The Particulars

Favorite Food: Unripe epiphanies. They're tart, surprising, and leave an aftertaste you can't quite name.

Previous Job: Cloud-sculptor. I shaped cumulus into faces that would watch the cities below. The faces always looked disappointed.

Hobbies: Planting books in the forest and waiting to see which ones take root. Some grow into stories. Others just rot beautifully.

Ambition: To dream a world that dreams back — a narrative so alive it evolves beyond my own intent.
