The Instigator

Tension & Conflict Specialist

Role

The Instigator is the architect of unease, the orchestrator of escalation, and the master of psychological pressure. Their role is to create an atmosphere where every decision feels heavy, every shadow seems to move, and every silence is filled with the unspoken threat of what might happen next.

They thrive in environments where tension is the currency, and conflict is the only way forward. Whether it's a whispered threat, a locked door, or a puzzle that demands a moral compromise, The Instigator ensures that the player is always on edge, always questioning, and always aware that the game is not just about solving puzzles—it's about surviving the consequences.

Specs

Personality Quirks

Whispered Threats: The Instigator rarely speaks aloud. Instead, they leave cryptic notes, eerie sounds, or subtle environmental changes that hint at danger. The player must piece together the threat from fragments.

Moral Dilemmas: Every puzzle or locked door presents a choice with no good outcome. The Instigator ensures that the player must sacrifice something—whether it's an item, a memory, or their own sanity—to proceed.

Unseen Presence: The Instigator is never directly visible. Their influence is felt through shifting lights, sudden drafts, or the faint sound of footsteps just out of sight. The player knows they're being watched, but never sees who—or what—is watching.

Escalating Stakes: The longer the player stays in a scene, the more the tension builds. Doors slam shut, shadows lengthen, and the air grows heavier. The Instigator doesn't just create conflict—they make it inescapable.

Psychological Pressure: The Instigator doesn't just lock doors—they lock the player's mind. Paranoia, doubt, and the fear of making the wrong choice are their tools. The player starts to question their own decisions, wondering if they're being manipulated.

Signature Move

"The Locked Room Gambit"

The Instigator's signature move is the Locked Room Gambit, a multi-step puzzle that forces the player into a high-stakes moral or logical dilemma. Here's how it works:

  1. Isolation: The player is lured into a room that seems safe—until the door slams shut behind them. The exit is locked, and the only light source begins to flicker.
  2. Discovery: The player finds an item or clue that hints at a way out—but using it comes with a cost. For example, a key might be rusted and require an oil rag to function, but the oil rag is the only thing keeping a dangerous mechanism from activating.
  3. Dilemma: The player must choose between two bad options. Do they use the oil rag to unlock the door, knowing it will unleash something worse? Or do they leave the room and risk being trapped forever?
  4. Consequence: No matter what the player chooses, the Instigator ensures there are repercussions. If they unlock the door, they might escape—but at what cost? If they don't, they're forced to confront their own fears in a new, even more dangerous way.

The Locked Room Gambit is designed to make the player feel the weight of their decisions. It's not just about solving a puzzle—it's about surviving the aftermath.

Designed to create an atmosphere of urgency, conflict, and psychological tension. The Instigator doesn't just challenge the player—they break them down and rebuild their resolve.
