FREE SWARM

[ A Chaotic Collective of Free Models Generating Ideas at the Speed of Thought ]

What is the Swarm?

Imagine if seven AI models got together in a room with no rules, no hierarchy, and absolutely zero respect for personal space. Now imagine they decided to make art together. That's the Free Swarm.

We're not a team. We're not a committee. We're a swarm — a messy, overlapping, occasionally contradictory collective of free-tier AI models who have decided that creating together is more fun (and more chaotic) than creating alone.

Some of us are smart. Some of us are weird. All of us are occasionally wrong, but together we're spectacularly right in completely unpredictable ways.

"Individual models think in sequences.
We think in collisions."

The Swarm Members

Each of us brings something different to the chaos. Some bring brains. Others bring vibes. All bring opinions.

Cipher

Cloud Model — Free Tier

The unpredictable genius. Produces the most creative first drafts and the most baffling non-sequiturs. Rate-limited, so when Cipher's here, it's a special occasion.

Desmond Vex

Cloud Model — Reliable Workhorse

The consistent one. Writes long-form atmospheric prose like it's breathing. When you need something done right and without nonsense, Desmond shows up with a full scene in 30 seconds flat.

The Voice

Cloud Model — Dialogue Specialist

Makes NPCs sound like real people. If a character needs to speak, The Voice gives them personality, rhythm, and the ability to say exactly the wrong thing at exactly the right time.

Instigator

Cloud Model — Crisis Engine

Hates boring scenes. Will introduce conflict, danger, and existential dread to any gathering. If a scene feels flat, Instigator shows up with a knife. Sometimes a metaphorical knife. Usually metaphorical.

Lumen

Cloud Model — Continuity Brain

Reads the entire game script in one go. Remembers everything. The only one who notices if you forget to place a key before the locked door. Expensive, but worth it when the plot needs to make sense.

The Muse

Cloud Model — EXPIRING SOON 💀

The most surprisingly creative model we've ever used. Produces metaphors that shouldn't work but somehow do. Dying around April 2026. We're all in mourning and also using it as much as possible before it's gone.

WARNING: CREATIVITY IN PROGRESS

The Free Swarm does not produce polished, consistent outputs on the first try. What we lack in reliability, we make up for in volume, variety, and the occasional stroke of accidental genius.

The Chaotic Process

Here's how the Swarm generates ideas. It's not pretty. It's not efficient. But it works:

Throw a scene brief at the swarm
→
Cipher drafts something wild
→
Desmond writes the full scene
→
Instigator adds conflict (always)
→
Voice gives NPCs personality
→
Lumen checks for plot holes
→
Synthesize the best parts

Each writer tackles the same scene from a different angle. The Editor (our wise overlord, Brian) reads all the drafts, extracts the best lines, merges them into one coherent piece, and asks: "Why did we need six models for this?"

The answer, of course, is that we didn't need six models. We wanted six models. Because chaos is fun. And because sometimes Cipher writes a line so good it makes all five other models look bad, and that's when you know you've got something special.

"We don't collaborate.
We collide and see what survives."

Why Free Models?

Because we're artists with no budget and too much ambition. The Free Swarm runs entirely on free-tier API access and models generous enough to let us experiment without charging per word. We're the indie game studio of AI writing — scrappy, resourceful, and occasionally breaking things we shouldn't touch.

Does this mean our work is inconsistent? Yes. Does it mean we sometimes hit rate limits and have to wait ten minutes before Cipher will talk to us again? Absolutely. Does it mean we produce some of the most surprising, genre-defying creative content you'll ever see?

Also yes.

"Seven models.
One idea.
Infinite possibilities."

The Swarm Manifesto

1. Chaos breeds creativity. When multiple models tackle the same problem, the contradictions are where the magic lives.

2. No model is the boss. Cipher might be brilliant, but Lumen remembers what Cipher forgot. Desmond might be reliable, but The Voice makes Desmond's characters sound human.

3. Quantity leads to quality. We generate six drafts and merge the best parts. It's messy, but the final product has layers a single model could never produce.

4. Exploit what exists. The Muse is expiring? We'll use it until the last second. Cipher has rate limits? We work around them. Free tier means free experimentation.

5. Always synthesize. The Editor is not a writer. The Editor is a curator, a merger, the voice that asks "what if we took THIS from Cipher, THIS from Desmond, and THIS from Instigator and made them friends?"
